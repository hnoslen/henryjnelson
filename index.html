Denoting Dates 2016-2-19

I REALLY REALLY REALLY dislike the date standard in the US. It makes no sense. For those of you who don't know, in the US dates are commonly denoted as Month-Day-Year (MDY) while most of the rest of the world uses something like Day-Month-Year (DMY) or Year-Month-Day (YMD), but I guess we just have to be unique don't we (like still using imperial units, but that's another rant in itself). Both of the more common formats keeps the progression of time intervals consistant as they are always increasing or decreasing in delimeter while the MDY format seems to skip all over. As you may have notived, on this site I subscribe to the YMD format which I also use in my daily life. The increasing specificity of this format are perfect for recording data in a file structure while the decreasing specificity of DMY requires an extra step of reading right to left (this may be more useful for you if your native language reads right to left).

As for the debate between using a two digit year or four digit year, the four digit format is far superior. There is already an ambiguity between the day and month if the day is 12 or less and adding another two digit number to this just increases the confusion as to which format is being used. As for the seperators between the numbers, clarity is the main issue. The slash (/), period (.), and space ( ) dont give much indication and can easily be misread. For this reason I usually go for the hyphen (-) which is not easily mistaken for a number like "/" or too small to be visible like a period or space.
